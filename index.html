Part 1 — Foundations
TOPIC 01
The 41 Qualities (Gunas) of the Universe
Gurvadi · Paradi · Vaisheshik · Adhyatmik
▼

Ayurveda believes everything in the universe — including our bodies — can be described by 41 specific qualities. These are split into four groups:

1. 10 Opposites (Gurvadi) — Physical qualities in pairs
Heavy / Light
Describes the weight or density of a substance.
Cold / Hot
The thermal nature of food or tissue.
Dry / Oily
The moisture content of a substance.
+ 7 more pairs
Such as Dull/Sharp, Dense/Liquid, Stable/Mobile, Gross/Subtle, Smooth/Rough, Solid/Flowing, Hard/Soft.
2. 10 Logical Qualities (Paradi) — Thinking & action

These include reasoning, counting, combining, and practicing. They govern cognitive and volitional processes.

3. 5 Sensory Qualities (Vaisheshik) — Through the senses
Sight (Form) Taste Smell Touch Space / Sound
4. 6 Spiritual Qualities (Adhyatmik) — Inner drives of the mind
Desire Happiness Suffering Effort Intellect (right/wrong) + Memory
TOPIC 02
The Three Mental Energies (Trigunas)
Satva · Rajas · Tamas
▼

Our minds are controlled by three distinct energies that shape personality and are directly influenced by the food we eat.

⬜ SATVA — Purity
Represents peace and wisdom.
Boosted by: Fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains.
🔴 RAJAS — Passion
Represents ambition and restlessness.
Fed by: Caffeine, spicy dishes, fried foods.
⬛ TAMAS — Inertia
Represents laziness and confusion.
Caused by: Red meat, leftovers, processed junk, alcohol.
TOPIC 03
The 5 Layers of the Body (Koshas)
Annamaya · Pranamaya · Manomaya · Vijnanamaya · Anandamaya
▼

A human being is made of five layers or "sheaths" — from the outermost physical to the deepest spiritual.

  1. 1.
    Annamaya (Physical) — Your actual body, built by the food you eat and maintained by exercise.
  2. 2.
    Pranamaya (Energy/Breath) — Your life force; controls breathing and blood circulation.
  3. 3.
    Manomaya (Mental) — Processes thoughts, emotions, joys, and sorrows.
  4. 4.
    Vijnanamaya (Wisdom) — Intellect and higher consciousness; deep understanding and reflection.
  5. 5.
    Anandamaya (Bliss) — Deepest layer of pure joy and peace, connected to the universe.

TOPIC 05
Waste Products (Malas)
Purisha · Mutra · Sweda · Dhatu Mala
▼

To stay healthy, the body must constantly flush out waste and toxins.

Purisha (Stool)
Removes solid waste from the bowels; keeps the gut healthy.
Mutra (Urine)
Removes liquid waste; balances body fluids; keeps kidneys working.
Sweda (Sweat)
Cools the body down; pushes toxins out through the skin.
Dhatu Mala (Tissue Waste)
Minor wastes produced by the 7 tissues — eye crust, ear wax, fingernails, hair.
TOPIC 06
Body Types (Prakriti)
Vata · Pitta · Kapha — set at birth by the Doshas
▼

Your unique physical and mental makeup is determined at birth based on the combination of the three Doshas.

VATA Air + Space
Build: Thin, light.
Strengths: Creative, quick learner, enthusiastic.
Tendencies: Dry skin, cold hands, anxiety, variable energy.
PITTA Fire + Water
Build: Medium, muscular.
Strengths: Sharp intellect, ambitious, focused.
Tendencies: Hot, impatient, anger-prone.
KAPHA Earth + Water
Build: Solid, heavy.
Strengths: Calm, loving, stable, loyal.
Tendencies: Weight gain, sluggishness, resistance to change.
People can also be dual types (Vata-Pitta, Pitta-Kapha, Vata-Kapha) or a perfect balance of all three — called Tridoshic.
TOPIC 07
How to Balance Your Body Type
Diet & lifestyle adjustments for each Dosha
▼

The principle is opposites balance — counter your Dosha's natural qualities with opposing foods and routines.

VATA Cold + Dry → Warm + Oily
Eat: Warm, oily, cooked foods — soups, stews, sesame oil.
Lifestyle: Grounding yoga, strict daily routine, early bedtime.
PITTA Hot + Sharp → Cool + Mild
Eat: Cooling mild foods — cucumbers, leafy greens, coconut. Avoid spicy food.
Lifestyle: Time in nature, relaxation, avoid midday heat.
KAPHA Heavy + Slow → Light + Stimulating
Eat: Light, spicy, stimulating foods. Avoid heavy dairy and sweets.
Lifestyle: Vigorous exercise, no daytime naps, variety and challenge.

TOPIC 09
The 20 Food Qualities in Nutrition
10 pairs of opposing qualities used to heal the body
▼

Ayurveda looks at 20 specific physical qualities (10 pairs of opposites) to select healing foods and herbs.

Heavy ↔ Light Cold ↔ Hot Oily ↔ Dry Smooth ↔ Rough Stable ↔ Mobile Gross ↔ Subtle Dense ↔ Liquid Soft ↔ Hard Dull ↔ Sharp Solid ↔ Flowing
How it works: If you have too much heat (Pitta excess), use a "cool" herb like Vetiver. If you have too much wetness/congestion (Kapha excess), use a "dry" spice like Turmeric. Examples: Ashwagandha is Heavy; Cucumber is Cold; Sesame oil is Oily; Aloe vera is Smooth; Rice is Stable.
TOPIC 10
The Digestive Fire (Agni)
13 types — Jatharagni · Dhatvagni · Bhutagni
▼

Agni is the internal "fire" that digests food and converts it into energy. There are 13 types in total.

1 × Jatharagni
The main fire in the stomach and intestines. Digests every meal you eat. The master Agni — all others depend on its strength.
7 × Dhatvagni
One fire per Dhatu. Converts digested food into the 7 body tissues — plasma → blood → muscle → fat → bone → marrow → reproductive tissue.
5 × Bhutagni
One fire per element. Processes the 5 natural elements (Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Space) within the body.

TOPIC 15
The Post-Digestive Effect (Vipaka)
How food affects the body AFTER digestion — beyond the immediate taste
▼

Rasa = the immediate taste on your tongue. Vipaka = the effect on the body after the food has been fully digested. The same taste can have a different post-digestive effect.

Sweet Vipaka
Builds bodily strength; nourishes and expands tissues.
Sour Vipaka
Enhances digestive efficiency and nutrient absorption.
Pungent Vipaka
Stimulates circulation and speeds up metabolism.

TOPIC 17
The 8 Principles of Ayurvedic Meals
Eight rules for selecting and eating food
▼
  1. 1.
    Prakruti (Nature) — The food's natural qualities: heavy or light, hot or cold.
  2. 2.
    Karan (Processing) — How you cook it: steaming, frying, adding spices changes the food's properties.
  3. 3.
    Samyoga (Combination) — Mixing the right foods together so they digest well and don't create toxic combinations.
  4. 4.
    Rashi (Quantity) — Eating the correct portion size so you don't overeat.
  5. 5.
    Desha (Place) — Eating local and seasonal foods from your region.
  6. 6.
    Kala (Time) — Eating at the right time of day and adjusting for the current season.
  7. 7.
    Upayoga Samstha (Rules for Use) — Eating in a calm environment and chewing well.
  8. 8.
    Upayokta (Consumer) — Choosing foods that fit your unique body type (Prakriti).
TOPIC 18
The Right Time, Condition & Portions to Eat
When and how much — Ayurvedic meal rules
▼
Timing

Your biggest, heaviest meal should be lunch (11 AM – 2 PM) when the digestive fire (Jatharagni) is strongest. Dinner should be the lightest meal of the day.

Mental Condition

Never eat when stressed, upset, or distracted — your body will not digest the food properly and Ama (toxic waste) will be formed.

Portion Size — The 1/3 Rule
Imagine your stomach in three equal parts.
→ Fill 1/3 with solid food
→ Fill 1/3 with liquid
→ Leave 1/3 completely empty for the food to churn properly.

Stop eating when you are 80% full.

The Taste Sequence for a Meal
The order of 6 tastes optimises digestion
▼

The order in which you eat the 6 tastes during a meal directly changes how well you digest it.

  1. 1st
    Sweet — Prepares and primes the digestive system.
  2. 2nd
    Sour + Salty — Gets the digestive juices fully flowing.
  3. 3rd
    Pungent (Spicy) + Bitter — Boosts metabolism and burns through the food.
  4. Last
    Astringent — Balances the palate and helps the body absorb all nutrients.
TOPIC 20
Incompatible Food Combinations (Virudhaharam)
16 types of bad combinations that create Ama (toxic waste)
▼

Mixing the wrong foods confuses the digestive system and creates Ama (toxic metabolic waste) in the body. There are 16 categories of incompatibility including seasonal mismatches, wrong cooking methods, and mixing hot and cold.

Common Toxic Combinations to Know
⚠ Never combine these:
• Honey + Ghee in equal parts
• Milk + sour fruits (e.g., milk with mango or banana)
• Fish + dairy products
• Eating out of season for your body type
• Mixing hot and cold items improperly (e.g., ice water with a hot meal)

Regularly doing this causes gas, bloating, and eventually chronic disease.
